Tiger

With an incredible strength, loud roar, imposing appearance and unparallel beauty, Tigers are the largest cats in the world, easily recognized by their distinctive coat with the characteristic pattern of dark stripes.

  • Scientific Name: Panthera tigris.
  • Average length and weight: Males average 108 inches (270 cm) in total length and weigh between 150–195 kg (331–430 lb), while females average 96 inches (240 cm) and 100–130 kg (220–290 lb).
  • Lifespan: between 20 and 26 years, the oldest recorded captive tiger lived for 26 years.
  • Habitat: Tigers occupy a variety of habitats from tropical forests, evergreen forests, woodlands and mangrove swamps to grasslands, savannah and rocky country. 13 countries where you can find wild tigers are: Bangladesh, Vietnam, Cambodia, India, Bhutan, Thailand, Indonesia, Laos, China, Malaysia, Russia, Nepal and Myanmar.

The tiger (Panthera tigris) is the largest cat species, most recognisable for their pattern of dark vertical stripes on reddish-orange fur with a lighter underside. The species is classified in the genus Panthera with the lion, leopard, jaguar and snow leopard.
